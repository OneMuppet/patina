# Patina — Brand

## The name
**Patina** is the prized sheen that forms on copper and bronze as they age — a
finish you can't buy, only earn through time and use. That's the promise: your
notes are plain files that accrue value the longer you keep them. The app is the
thin, living layer over them.

## Core values
**Clean · Hyper-efficient · No bullshit.**
Everything below is downstream of these three. If a design choice doesn't serve
clarity, speed, or honesty, it's cut.

## The system: Ink + Copper
Patina is **ink on paper** with exactly **one accent: copper** — the warm metal
that *becomes* the patina. Copper marks only what is interactive or alive: the
caret, selection, links, wiki-links, and the structural token in code. No second
UI accent. No gradients. Deliberately not the teal/mint/purple that every other
tool reached for — a warmer, older, quieter palette.

| Role | Light | Dark |
|---|---|---|
| **Copper** (the one accent) | `#B5611F` | `#E09957` |
| Ink / Paper | system `labelColor` / `textBackgroundColor` | (adapts) |

### Syntax palette — aged metals
Code uses a restrained set of weathered-metal tones (copper, bronze, brass,
oxblood) instead of the usual blue/green/purple. Warm, earthy, distinct.

Spaced far apart in hue *and* value so tokens read at a glance.

| Token | Tone | Light | Dark |
|---|---|---|---|
| keys / structure | copper (accent) | `#B5611F` | `#E09957` |
| strings / content | olive / sage | `#577A2B` | `#ABCF78` |
| numbers | brass / gold | `#99780F` | `#E6C770` |
| keywords (`true`/`false`/`null`) | oxblood | `#BA3326` | `#ED7869` |
| comments | `tertiaryLabelColor` | — | — |
| punctuation | `secondaryLabelColor` | — | — |

## Typography
- **UI & prose:** the system font (SF Pro). Native, neutral, no personality tax.
- **Code:** SF Mono.
- **Hierarchy comes from weight and size, never boxes or borders.**
- Editor prose is left-aligned and wraps at a readable measure; code fills the pane.

## Design principles
1. **Plain files first.** The UI is a lens over a folder you own, never a cage.
2. **One accent, used sparingly.** Copper marks what's alive; the rest is ink on paper.
3. **Native & instant.** System materials, real `NSTextView`, no WebView, no chrome for chrome's sake.
4. **Type does the work.** Weight and space create hierarchy.
5. **Quiet by default.** Color appears only where it carries meaning.
6. **Earn the patina.** Nothing flashy; refinement that reveals itself with use.

## Voice
Warm, brief, low-ego. Short declaratives. No marketing fog. "Quiet, quick, to the
bone." Credit the craft, not the hype.

## Logo — Strata
The mark is **strata**: stacked rounded bars on a flat **ink** field. They read two
ways at once — *lines of text* (notes) and *layers of patina* accruing over time.
Three bars in warm paper, the top one in **copper** (the single accent). Ragged
widths so it reads as writing, not a menu. Flat, no gloss, no gradient.
(Generated by `tools/build_icon.sh`; mark `strata`.)

## Do / Don't
- ✅ Ink on paper; one copper accent, used sparingly.
- ✅ Flat color, left-aligned text, generous whitespace, system fonts.
- ❌ No teal/mint/purple, no second UI accent, no gradients in the UI.
- ❌ No drop shadows or borders/boxes where weight and space will do.
- ❌ Never claim a number you haven't measured.
